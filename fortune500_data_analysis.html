US Fortune500 2025 Data Analysis

In this project, we grab the raw dataset via Web Scraping from 50pros.com website. Web Scraping is done by manually inspect the respective web page and copy-paste it into a text file. Then, I put it in my local webserver and successfully grab the table using Pandas module of Python directly into usable Dataframe.

Out of 10 Columns/Fields and 500 rows of raw data, I try to dig deep into various insights and, turns out, many many insights I can come up with.

Turns out, as we can see, from the Heatmap Chart, there are several parameters that have pretty close correlation with each other, for example: relative_humidity_2m and vapour_pressure_deficit, dew_point_2m and temperature_80m.

The complete correlation conclusion:

  • 'relative_humidity_2m' has correlation of -0.86 to 'vapour_pressure_deficit'.
  • 'temperature_80m' has correlation of 0.92 to 'temperature_2m'.
  • 'temperature_80m' has correlation of 0.86 to 'soil_temperature_0cm'.
  • 'temperature_80m' has correlation of 0.71 to 'dew_point_2m'.
  • 'temperature_2m' has correlation of 0.92 to 'soil_temperature_0cm'.
  • 'dew_point_2m' has correlation of 0.81 to 'relative_humidity_2m' parameter if temperature_2m > 18C, compared to 0.56 with all data included.
  • 'cloud_cover_mid' has correlation of 0.51 to 'precipitation_probability'.
  • 'cloud_cover_low' has correlation of 0.52 to 'relative_humidity_2m'
  • 'soil_moisture_0_to_1cm' has correlation of 0.59 to 'relative_humidity_2m'.
  • 'soil_temperature_0cm has correlation of 0.63 to 'dew_point_2m'.
  • 'vapour_pressure_deficit' has correlation of 0.65 to 'temperature_2m'.


Our most concern about weather is how big the precipitation_probability is. The bigger the precipitation_probability, the bigger the chance we'll have rain that will give fresh water to our environment. One of our conclusion from the analysis is when relative_humidity_2m reaches maximum, the precipitation_probability is one of the highest also.

relative_humidity_2m has connection also with soil_moisture_0_to_1cm, as can be seen in the Hourly Precipitation Probability below.

Maybe, it's due to partial condensation phenomenon, so when the precipitation probability is relatively stable (almost flat), the soil moisture is still fluctuating. This phenomenon is used in Fog Farming to supply fresh water in arid/semi-arid area by using a lot of nets to help water vapour condensation.

General conclusion on precipitation_probability in Nashville:

  • precipitation_probability reaches maximum hourly average value at 6pm Nashville time (GMT-5)
  • precipitation_probability reaches maximum hourly maximum value between 12am to 4am Nashville

Hourly Average Precipitation Probability in Las Vegas is slighly different than in Nashville:

When relative_humidity_2m fluctuates, the soil_moisture_0_to_1cm is almost flat. Besides that, relative_humidity_2m in Nashville is far bigger than in Las Vegas (almost 3-to-1 ratio)

Comparison between Nashville and Las Vegas weather data:

  • Max temperature_2m of Las Vegas (41.32C) is higher than Nashville (30.85C).
  • Min temperature_2m of Las Vegas (6.42C) is higher than Nashville (-2.80C). This is a bit different than usual knowledge of desert-like places.
  • Max precipitation_probability in Las Vegas (70%) is lower than in Nashville (100%).
  • Min precipitation_probability in Las Vegas and Nashville is the same (0%).
  • Max Daily Average of Precipitation Probability in Las Vegas (25.83% on 4 May 2025) is roughly half of in Nashville (54.83% on 6 Apr 2025).
  • Max Monthly Average values of Precipitation Probability in Las Vegas (2.29% on May 2025) is lower than in Nashville (8.83% on May 2025).
  • Max Hourly Average values of Precipitation Probability in Las Vegas (2.11% at 23:00 GMT) is lower than in Nashville (10.94% at 23:00 GMT). In both locations, the maximum hourly average value happens in the afternoon at each local time.
  • Max Daily Maximum of Precipitation Probability in Las Vegas (70% at 5 May 2025) is significantly lower than in Nashville (100% at 6 April 2025).
  • Max Monthly Maximum of Precipitation Probability in Las Vegas (70% at May 2025) is significantly lower than in Nashville (100% in April 2025)
  • Max Hourly Maximum of Precipitation Probability in Las Vegas (70% at 23:00 GMT/16:00 PST) is significantly lower than in Nashville (100% at 6:00 GMT/1:00 CST).
  • Min Daily Minimum, Min Monthly Minimum and Min Hourly Minimum of Precipitation Probability in Las Vegas is the same as in Nashville, i.e 0%.

Comparison of relative_humidity_2m between Nashville and Las Vegas:

  • Max Daily Average of Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (80.7% on 6 May 2025) is a bit lower than in Nashville (96.04% at 6 April 2025)
  • Max Monthly Average of Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (21.09% on May 2025) is significantly lower than in Nashville (74.91% at May 2025)
  • Max Hourly Average of Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (26.91% at 13:00 GMT) is significantly lower than in Nashville (79.80% at 12:00 GMT)
  • Max Daily Maximum of Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (96% on 6 May 2025) is a bit lower than in Nashville (100% on 3,5 and 6 April and 3,6,8,13 and 20 May 2025).
  • Max Monthly Maximum Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (96% on May 2025) is a bit lower than in Nashville (96% on April and May 2025).
  • Max Hourly Maximum Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (96% at 13:00 GMT) is a bit lower than in Nashville (100% at 12:00 GMT).
  • Min Daily Minimum of Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (4% on 22, 23, and 24 May 2025) is significantly lower than in Nashville (19% on 16 April 2025).
  • Min Monthly Minimum Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (4% on May 2025) is significantly lower than in Nashville (19% on April 2025).
  • Min Hourly Minimum Relative Humidity 2m in Las Vegas (4% at 00:00 GMT) is significantly lower than in Nashville (19% at 23:00 GMT).
